Kalonji (Nigella sativa)



The Kalonji plant, also known as Nigella sativa, is an annual flowering plant that grows up to 20-30 cm in height. It has linear lanceolate leaves and produces white and pale blue flowers. The plant is native to southern Europe, southwestern Asia, and is naturalized in many parts of northern Africa. It is traditionally used as both medicine and as food in rice dishes, curries, dahl, and stews. The seeds of the plant have been used for their diuretic, antihypertensive, antidiabetic, anticancer, immunomodulatory, analgesic, antimicrobial, anthelmintics, analgesics, and anti-inflammatory properties

Saffron (Crocus sativus)



The Saffron plant, also known as Crocus sativus, is a fall-blooming perennial plant that grows from an underground corm or bulb. It has thin, blade-like green foliage that can grow up to 60 cm in height. The plant produces purple flowers with yellow stamens and red stigmas, which are harvested for use as a spice. Saffron is native to the Mediterranean region and is widely cultivated in many parts of the world. It is traditionally used as a spice, food coloring, and in traditional medicine

Zingiber (Ginger)



The Zingiber plant, also known as Zingiber officinale, is a tender herbaceous perennial plant in the ginger family. It has a thick, branched rhizome with brown outer skin and yellowish-green flesh. The plant produces narrow, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The flowers are yellowish-green and appear in dense, conical spikes, although they are rarely seen in cultivation. The Zingiber plant is native to Southeast Asia and is widely cultivated for its edible rhizome, commonly known as ginger.

Senna (Cassia angustifolia)



The Senna plant, also known as Cassia senna, is a flowering plant that grows up to 1-2 meters in height. It has pinnate leaves with four to eight pairs of leaflets and produces bright yellow flowers. The plant is native to tropical Africa and is naturalized in many parts of the world. It is traditionally used as a laxative and in the treatment of various health conditions. The leaves and pods of the plant are used as medicine, and the plant is also used as an ornamental in gardens

Ajwain (Trachyspermum ammi)
